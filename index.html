About Me

Who I Am

I’m Lewis Kimari — a Kenyan software developer, cybersecurity learner, Android developer, data analyst, and creative technologist driven by one mission: to use technology to reduce unemployment and create opportunities for others. The GCGO that resonates most with me is Unemployment, not just as a global challenge but as a lived reality in my community. Every skill I build is part of a larger vision: securing my own future while empowering others to unlock theirs.

My technical journey began at Modcom, where I trained across the full stack using HTML, CSS, Python, JavaScript, Node.js, React, Express, Flask, MongoDB, and SQL. I later expanded into Android development, AI/ML exploration, UI/UX design, and data analysis — skills that strengthened my ability to build intelligent, intuitive, and user‑centered digital experiences.

Today, I’m transitioning into Cybersecurity, a field that aligns perfectly with my curiosity, discipline, and desire to protect digital systems. Through ALX’s 7‑month Cybersecurity program, I’m sharpening my ability to secure applications, understand threats, and contribute to a safer digital economy. My long‑term path leads toward becoming a Cybersecurity Analyst, then a Penetration Tester, and eventually a Security Engineer or CISO — roles that allow me to solve real problems while creating opportunities for others.

Alongside my tech journey, I am also pursuing a Bachelor of Arts degree at the University of Nairobi, where I plan to major in Psychology and Sociology. Understanding human behavior, social systems, and community dynamics strengthens my ability to design secure, human‑centered solutions and supports my mission to address unemployment through both technology and social impact.

What makes me unique is the blend of my technical and creative identities. I’m a full‑stack engineer, Android app builder, data analyst, 2D animator, UI/UX designer, and part‑time barber. This combination has taught me precision, storytelling, and how to connect with people from all walks of life. I also curate Spotify playlists, using BPM analysis and mood mapping to design emotional musical journeys. Whether I’m building secure systems, designing brand identities, analyzing data, crafting mobile apps, or giving someone a clean fade, I bring the same level of intentionality and craftsmanship.

I’m also the creator of The African Dream, a personal brand rooted in innovation, cultural identity, and the belief that African creativity can compete on any global stage. Through design, storytelling, and technology, I aim to build solutions that uplift communities, celebrate African excellence, and inspire others to dream boldly.

I believe in the power of minimalism, functional code, and secure design to solve complex problems. As I grow in cybersecurity and expand my technical range, I’m building a career that allows me to live out my passion: using creativity, discipline, and technology to make a real impact — one system, one project, and one person at a time.

And if you ever need a fun fact: I’m probably the only developer you’ll meet who can debug code, analyze data, curate a playlist, build an Android app, and fix a hairline in the same afternoon.
